# **`Background`**

Data center computing has long been a big consumer of energy, but cryptocurrency and generative AI has led to a surging increase in energy demand. This increase threatens any gains from newly built renewable energy sources as [fossil fuel plants are being kept online to meet this demand](https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/us-grid-operator-pjm-asks-talen-energy-postpone-fossil-fuel-plant-retirements-2024-01-10/). 

The increasing energy demand from data centers also strains the electrical grid, [resulting in less reliable power delivery](https://www.bloomberg.com/graphics/2024-ai-power-home-appliances/) (e.g. electrical fires and blackouts) and [higher costs for consumers](https://www.utilitydive.com/news/pjm-board-transmission-rtep-maryland-dominion-firstenergy/702263/). Data centers also draw large amounts of water from the communities where they are built to cool their equipment and introduce pollutants via wastewater. 

Currently, data centers are not required to disclose projected impacts to the public and regular consumers have no protection from those impacts. 

# **`The Bill`**

The New York State Sustainable Data Centers Act mandates that:

- Data centers with a 5+ megawatt capacity must disclose their proposed location, use of labor, projected energy and water usage, greenhouse gas emissions, and energy sources (e.g. fossil fuel vs. renewable sources) prior to being built 
- Data centers that do not comply with the disclosure requirement shall be fined up to $10,000 with an additional $10,000 per day they are not in compliance. 
- Data centers must hold public hearings describing the community impacts associated with the information disclosed prior to being built, including the disclosure of projected jobs created within the host community 
- Data centers must meet energy consumption efficiency goals set by the New York Public Authority 
- Data centers must commit to 100% renewable energy sources by 2040 
- Data centers are prohibited from getting discounts on energy from fossil fuels through “power purchase agreements” (bulk power purchasing from power utilities) 
The New York Public Service Commission establishes a community discount plan for low income and moderate-income residents within data center host communities
